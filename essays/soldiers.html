What the Heck Are Soldiers Even Fighting For?

Published: February 27, 2024 Section: Essays Time: 5 min read
Tags: politics, soldiers, war, conflict, political commentary, propaganda

Politics are filthy, corrupt, and self-serving. But the soldiers fighting on the frontlines are just ordinary people; they have families, friends, lovers. They weren’t born to be killers. Society tends to oversimplify everything, reducing conflicts to “good vs evil.” But when you step back and see things from different angles, that’s when you start breaking free from those narrow views. War is never as simple as "us vs them."

For so long, we’ve looked at one side of the coin. We see Russian soldiers as enemies, but to the politicians, they're just numbers—pawns in a larger chess game. Are they really anything more?

From an Afghan’s perspective, a Russian soldier has always been the cruel enemy—ruthless, deserving death. But when you step back and look at their side, when you explore the lives of those we call enemies, study their psychology, listen to their music and poems, and feel their emotions, it becomes clear. These soldiers we perceive as monsters are just like us, trapped in the same tangled web. It’s the politicians—those invisible figures, the top one percent of the top one percent—who pull the strings. They sit behind their desks, safe from the battlefields they've never seen, turning us into ruthless creatures, pitting us against one another.

Think about what happened after the Soviet war. Look at the 20-year battle between soldiers and the Taliban. None of those soldiers wanted to slaughter their fellow brothers. Yet, politicians fanned the flames, turning ordinary people into bloodthirsty animals. In the context of Afghanistan, government troops or Taliban, both were just numbers in this relentless game played in the name of religion and country.

Every soldier, if given the chance, would choose a normal life—being with their family, having kids, a steady job, peace. No one truly wants to kill their own if they had a choice. That’s what I’ve always believed, and nothing changes that for me. Whether it’s Taliban fighters, Afghan soldiers, U.S. soldiers, or Russian soldiers, ANYONE, I don’t blame them. It’s the politicians, the true puppet masters, the self-appointed gods, who are to blame. They make rules and use people as disposable pieces in their power struggles. Talking about them feels like discussing a conspiracy so vast, it's beyond our grasp.

In the end, the harsh reality is that every soldier would prefer a life of normalcy if given the option—cherishing moments with their family, enjoying peace, and pursuing dreams. It’s the political elite, hidden away from the chaos of real battlefields, who turn the lives of ordinary people into a relentless cycle of violence. Their manipulation and greed force us to confront a stark truth: the true enemy is not the soldier on the frontlines, but the faceless architects of war.
