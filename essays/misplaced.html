What the F*** Is Wrong with This Society?

Published: October 13, 2024 Section: Essays Time: 7 min read
Tags: society, values, wedding, education, celebrity

What the f*** is wrong with this society? Obsessing over wedding parties, showing off how much money was wasted, and measuring value by how expensive the event was. For weeks now, everyone has been yapping about the wedding of Rashid Khan, an Afghan cricketer — as if it’s some world-changing event. It’s a wedding, I know. So what? SO WHAT? What’s the big deal? Why the obsession? And then you see these fully grown men travel from far-flung places just to gossip about it. Is this what human lives have come to? Wasting time glorifying meaningless displays of wealth and status?

Celebrity culture is a sickness — a pathetic distraction for minds too weak to think for themselves. If only this much attention were given to scientists, engineers, or educators, the world might be a better place. But no, society worships athletes and celebrities as if they’re gods. I don’t mean in any way that sports are unnecessary? They’re fine. But at least people SHOULD keep a balance. Where’s the attention to innovation, science, and technology? Where’s the outrage when 35,000 people flock to a wedding, yet not even 1,000 stand up against the ban on innocent girls' education in Afghanistan? Millions are poured into cricket by a country like Afghanistan, while their education system is crumbling. Pathetic. Absolutely pathetic.

The hypocrisy is staggering. The priorities are rotten. Education, progress, and justice are cast aside so people can drool over celebrities living lives they will never have. This is not admiration; it’s mindless worship. A mass delusion.

And then look at these social media addicts — yapping louder than ever. Rashid Khan’s wedding, celebrity gossip, who wore what, who married whom. What the f***? Can’t these people find something better to do than live vicariously through the lives of others? This obsession with celebrity culture is nothing but a reflection of empty lives. These people are mentally weak, spiritually bankrupt. They’ve lost all sense of what matters. They have no goals. No ambitions. No purpose. All they are left with is just mindless consumption. Consuming gossip. Consuming trends. Consuming lives that aren’t theirs.

They never stop to think about creating value or producing something meaningful? These ideas sound like Alien language to them. All they care about is impressing others with hollow words, fake opinions, and recycled thoughts. Their entire existence revolves around projecting an image — an image they think will earn them a place among their equally shallow peers.

These brain rotten creatures are trapped. Trapped in their tiny, pathetic worlds, unable to see beyond their noses. Thinking universally? Impossible. Changing their minds? Never. Their entire worldview is dictated by the herd. If the majority believes it, they follow. They don’t question. They don’t challenge. They just follow. Like sheep. Pathetic sheep. They hear something, spread it without thought, and pat themselves on the back for being "informed." Yet, there’s no substance. No critical thinking. No depth. Just shallow opinions and loud mouths.

They are so obsessed with praising themselves and their superficial perspectives that there’s no room for real ideas, no space for meaningful conversations. Try to present a different view, and they’ll shut it down. Not because they’ve thought it through, but because thinking hurts. Because thinking would expose how empty their beliefs truly are.

This society, filled with these pathetic creatures, is just irreparable — rotten to the core. Who would even bother trying to fix it? Explaining real ideas to these people is like explaining calculus to a dog — completely pointless. They wouldn’t understand. They wouldn’t care. The herd doesn’t want truth. It wants comfort. It wants distractions. It wants to follow.
