I Just Don’t Really Understand Society’s Misplaced Values

Published: October 13, 2024 Section: Essays Time: 6 min read
Tags: society, values, wedding, education, culture

I just really don’t understand why society values things like wedding parties, how much one spends, and how expensive a party is. For the past few weeks, there’s been a wedding party of Rashid Khan, an Afghan cricketer, and everybody is talking about it. I just really don’t understand. It’s a wedding, okay, so what? What? Yeah, it’s a wedding, a personal thing, so what? And people are just really that much ozgar, coming from far, far places, yapping about this and that wedding. I really do not understand society's values. In my opinion, if we valued scientists and engineers that much instead of a random sportsman, the world would be so much better. I’m not against sports, but giving that much attention to it is pure nonsense. Okay, if you want to give sports that much attention, at least make a balance and give the same attention to education. But it’s just pure zalm (injustice) that 35,000 people can come to a wedding party in person, and not even 1,000 people can rise against the ban on girls' education in Afghanistan. At least keep the f***ing balance between sports and education. You won’t be able to do anything if you keep acting like this. People cannot go to school, there’s no budget for education, and yet millions are poured into cricket—that’s really nonsense, complete nonsense. Afghan society is messed up, and it’s just not repairable.

And then look at the people who yap even more about these things on social media: Rashid Khan’s wedding, this and that. What the f***, bro? For real? Can’t these people find anything better to do than talk about other people’s lives on social media? These people are sick. They have mental problems. They’ve lost their moral values and forgotten what’s necessary as humans in this world. They have no plans to realize, no goals or ambitions to pursue. So how am I supposed to fit in with these people? I can’t see my place among them. I can’t relate to their stories or their yapping. I can’t pursue a conversation with these people since their perspective is so different from mine. I don’t even know how these people live in this world. How the f*** do they survive? But that’s easy—they just consume. They never think about producing or contributing value to the world. They only think about themselves and impressing others. So how am I supposed to sit with them? How am I supposed to make them my friends? They are trapped in their little, pathetic minds and can’t even see reality. They don’t think universally, nor would they ever change their minds. They are so good at following—like sheep following their master—without ever knowing anything about the person or group. Their view of the world is solely based on the idea that if more people follow something, they will follow it too. They never check the source of the information they hear, yet they spread it to others and pride themselves on being intelligent. They would never give you a chance to express your ideas since they are too busy praising themselves, their ideas, and their “correct” perspective of the world.

This society, filled with these pathetic creatures, is just irreparable, beyond control. Who would repair it? To whom would you tell your vision of the world, which is like explaining calculus to a dog? In the end, I’m left with this: it’s me, only me. I can’t fit in with these people, and I would never try to. But to prove this idea to myself, I need to do something extraordinary. Only then will I be at peace, knowing that from the start, I said I couldn’t fit in with these sheep, and my perception was correct. So, yeah, no mixing with these pathetic sheep, but I need to prove this to myself by doing something extraordinary. See ya.
